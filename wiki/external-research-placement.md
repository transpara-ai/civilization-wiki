# External Research Placement

External Research Placement is the Stage 0 rule for deciding where competitor,
adjacent-technology, and complementary-technology research belongs.

The rule is based on the institutional owner of the research question:

```text
commercial product, sales, partner, market, or competitor question
  -> Transpara LLC Platform product knowledge

Civilization capability, agent tooling, memory, governance, provenance,
workflow, or autonomous-development question
  -> Civilization knowledge

cross-boundary decision, authority implication, handoff, or policy
  -> exchange boundary
```

## Why the rule exists

The same external organization or tool can matter in different ways. A product
comparison belongs with the LLC Platform product estate. A lesson about agent
memory, multi-agent review, provenance, or autonomous software development
belongs with Civilization research. A finding that changes what Transpara-AI may
promise, infer, or access belongs at the exchange boundary.

Without this placement rule, the wiki risks becoming a raw landing zone for
research. Stage 0 says the raw-to-published path should instead be:

```text
research branch
  -> normalized record
  -> curated finding
  -> wiki-input
  -> wiki presentation
```

## Sakana AI research set

The Stage 0 source places two Sakana AI reports under Civilization
external-landscape research:

| Research artifact | Placement reason |
| --- | --- |
| `[[sakana-ai-evaluation|TAI-RES-2026-001-v1.0.0-Sakana-AI-Evaluation.md]]` | Evaluates Sakana AI as an adjacent AI research/product organization and identifies learning opportunities for the Transpara-AI Civilization. |
| `[[sakana-ai-adjacent-landscape|TAI-RES-2026-002-v1.0.0-Sakana-AI-Adjacent-Landscape.md]]` | Extends the Sakana research set outward into collaborators, recursive self-improvement work, autonomous research systems, evolutionary AI tooling, and collective-intelligence orchestration. |

The institutional owner is Civilization external-landscape research because both
documents examine adjacent capability patterns for the Civilization. They are not
commercial Platform competitive intelligence and they are not customer/demo
access policy.

The second report preserves the deep-research workflow's adversarial-verification
posture: verified claims, medium-confidence claims, refuted claims, and known
gaps are all carried as research evidence. Its primary value to the wiki is not a
new doctrine claim; it is a map of external systems the Civilization may later
evaluate under a governed research-to-doctrine path.

These examples are not a universal classification of Sakana AI. They are
placement decisions for two specific research artifacts.
