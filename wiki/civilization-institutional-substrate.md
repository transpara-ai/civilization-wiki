# Civilization Institutional Substrate

The Civilization Institutional Substrate is the proposed frame for treating the
Transpara-AI Civilization as more than a software project or a Dark Factory
workstream. It names the surrounding institution: Transpara LLC, the commercial
Transpara Platform, the adjacent Transpara-AI Civilization, the exchange boundary
between human company and agent body, the OpenBrain and wiki knowledge pipeline,
and the local filesystem/migration substrate that gives the work a physical
shape.

This article is a wiki presentation of Stage 0 source material. It is not
accepted doctrine. The source scaffold is explicitly `status: proposal` and
`canonical: false`; it did not move `dark-factory`, alter runtime paths, change
services, steer active branches, or authorize production behavior.

## Why the substrate exists

The Stage 0 thesis is that the body of the system should match the ontology of
the institution:

```text
Transpara LLC
  human company, commercial owner, legal actor, product steward

Transpara Platform
  commercial industrial software product and runtime estate

Transpara-AI Civilization
  adjacent agent civilization that augments the humans

Exchange boundary
  formal handoff layer between company authority, agent action, product
  knowledge, demo runtime data, and customer confidentiality

Machine substrate
  caches, containers, package stores, toolchains, and local runtime support

Archive
  retired material with provenance, manifests, and recovery notes
```

The important shift is naming. [[dark-factory]] remains the formative production
doctrine, but the umbrella becomes Civilization: doctrine, governance,
operations, implementation, knowledge, memory, platform alignment, migration
tooling, and lineage.

## Stage 0 boundary

[[stage-0-institutional-substrate]] is preparation only. It creates a navigable
source set and provenance model for later decisions. It does not make the new
scaffold canonical and does not begin a filesystem or service migration.

The closeout source records the hard stop:

```text
no live canonical paths altered
no active development steered
verification passed
```

That boundary matters because a wiki article can otherwise make proposal text
feel more settled than it is. Here the proposal remains proposal. The wiki is a
read surface, not an authority source.
