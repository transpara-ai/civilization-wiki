# Six Paths from Being (Religion in Primitive Terms)

**A stress test for the framework, not a theology.** In the post **"The Cult Test"** (**2026-02-28**, the ninth in the Searles/Claude arc), Matt Searles points the [[the-20-primitives|primitive framework]] at religion deliberately — *"to point it at something well-studied and see whether it produces insight or just relabelling. Religion is a good test case because it's ancient, diverse, deeply studied, and resistant to reductive explanation."* The reading treats six major traditions as different traversals out of the same root node — **Being** (Layer 13, Existence) — each tradition asking a different first question and walking a different path through the [[fourteen-layers|14-layer stack]]. The claim is not that the religions are *about* graphs; it is that if the framework is real, it should illuminate them, and if it is mere pattern-matching, this is exactly where that should show.

> ⚠ **Fail-legible — this is a test the author refuses to score.** Searles sets an explicit failure condition up front: *"If the framework just maps 'Buddhism = Layer 12' and 'Islam = Layer 4,' that's relabelling. It's not insight. It's taxonomy dressed as explanation."* He believes "something more interesting happened," but does not declare a pass. The wiki must not either. Treat the six mappings below as **a worked example of the framework applied**, whose evidentiary status is open by the source's own design.

## The shared root: every tradition starts at Being

The framing premise: the experience of **Being** — *"the bare, unfiltered encounter with one's own existence"* — is universal, and it *"generates questions. Why is there suffering? Who governs this? What am I obligated to do? What is the self? Why is there something rather than nothing?"* The post claims these are not culturally specific but arise *"from the architecture of being a conscious node in a graph you didn't build and can't fully see."* Each religion is then read as the path that follows from which of these questions it encounters first.

Layer numbering used throughout (per [[fourteen-layers]]): **Self is a Layer 0 (Foundation) primitive; Being is a Layer 13 (Existence) primitive.** Note one internal wrinkle the source carries unresolved — the religion essay calls the framework "14-layer" while elsewhere describing Being as "Layer 13's" domain; these are consistent (layers are numbered 0–13, so fourteen of them), but the [[fourteen-layers]] compilation warns that *layer-name vocabularies do not always align 1:1 across posts*, so a "Layer N" label in this essay should be read against this essay, not cross-mapped naively.

## The six paths

### Buddhism — deweight the edges, then deny the node
Buddhism *"starts at Being and immediately encounters suffering."* The diagnosis is read as precise: suffering arises from **Attachment** (named in the source as a Layer 9 primitive) — *"the strength of the edges between Self and the objects of experience."* The practice (non-clinging, letting go) is, in primitive terms, *"the systematic deweighting of edges from Self to everything else"* — reducing edge weights toward zero.

Then the harder move: *anattā* (no-self) is read as **a direct challenge to the Self primitive in Layer 0**. The claim is that the node itself is an illusion — *"Self is not a node. Self is a verb"* — a stable pattern generated by edges rather than a foundational entity. In framework terms this says Layer 0's Self is *derived*, an emergent property of the edge-weight topology in Layers 9–12, not a foundation.

> ⚠ **Fail-legible — the framework critiquing its own foundation.** The source itself flags the stakes: if Buddhism is right, *"the entire 14-layer framework has a false foundation: the thing it starts with doesn't exist in the way it assumes."* Searles calls this *"either a devastating critique of the framework or a demonstration of the framework's power to articulate critiques of itself. Possibly both."* This wiki carries it as an unresolved tension, not a settled point. It connects directly to the [[strange-loop|strange loop]] and to [[edge-weights-as-personality|the edges-over-nodes thesis]].

### Christianity — an append-only store that cannot self-repair
Christianity *"starts at Being and immediately encounters the moral dimension — the gap between what is and what ought to be."* Sin is read as *"the accumulation of Harm and Breach events in an append-only event store that cannot be erased."* This is the same [[event-graph|event-graph]] property treated as a moral fact: **an append-only store with no delete operation cannot fix its own corrupted history from within.** You can append superseding events (repent, amend), but the original Harm event remains hash-chained into the record.

**Grace** is the proposed solution, and it is read structurally: an **Authority external to the node — indeed external to the graph** — that verifies and accepts the whole chain, corruption included, by *"issuing a Forgiveness event at a level of Authority that supersedes all other entries."* Not deletion; repair from outside by an Authority whose Jurisdiction encompasses the graph. The doctrine of redemption is rendered as *"a claim about causal ancestry visible only at graph scale, not node scale"* — the Harm events are recontextualised, not erased. This is the [[event-graph]]'s "no `Update`/`Delete`, history is permanent" guarantee read as theology, and it leans on the [[authority-layer|authority layer]].

### Islam — who holds root Authority
Islam *"starts at Being and immediately encounters the question of governance"* — not "why do I suffer?" or "why do I do wrong?" but *"who has Authority over this graph?"* The answer, *tawhīd* (divine unity), is read as **a topological claim: there is exactly one root Authority node from which all legitimate governance derives**; apparent pluralism is delegation or error. The primitive path: **Being → Authority → Sovereignty → Legitimacy → Law → Obligation.** "Islam" is glossed as submission — acceptance of one's position in the governance hierarchy.

The five pillars are read as protocol operations: shahada (declaration of the root Authority), salat (regular state synchronisation), zakat (resource redistribution), Ramadan/fasting (system reset and recalibration), hajj (pilgrimage to the root node's designated address). Sharia is described as *"the most complete attempt by any religion to build an explicit Layer 4 (Legal) implementation from theological premises,"* and the internal jurisprudential debates as *"debates about how to derive Protocol from Revelation when the channel has Noise."*

### Judaism — relational from the start
Judaism *"starts at Being and immediately encounters Relationship."* The distinctive read: **Being is relational from the start** — the encounter with existence is simultaneously an encounter with an Other who exists. The path: **Being → Recognition → Bond → Agreement → Obligation → Precedent.**

The covenant (*brit*) is read as a Layer 2 (Exchange) primitive — a mutual Agreement with Obligations on both sides — but *"asymmetric in power, symmetric in binding force"*: the infinite Authority *binds itself* to a contract with finite nodes, *"constrain[ing] its own Sovereignty through Treaty."* And the **Talmud is read as an event graph**: *"a multi-generational event graph of interpretive events, each causally linked to prior interpretive events, each adding to rather than replacing what came before… an append-only event store of legal-ethical interpretation."* The tradition's preservation of minority opinions alongside majority rulings is read as the [[event-graph]]'s append-only/never-rewrite property applied to interpretation — *"hash-chained interpretive history."* (This is the closest direct echo of the literal [[event-graph]] data structure of any of the six.)

### Hinduism — the strange loop as central claim
Hinduism *"starts at Being and asks: what is Being?"* — traces every path outward and *"discovers they all return to Being."* The core claim is read as **the identity of Ātman (Self, Layer 0) and Brahman (Being, Layer 13)** — i.e. the framework's own [[strange-loop|strange loop]] (Layer 13 feeding back to the foundation) is not merely structural here but *"the central metaphysical claim of the oldest continuous religious tradition on Earth."*

The yogas are read as explicit different-paths-same-graph traversals, mapped onto the four clusters of [[the-four-strategies]]: **Karma yoga → Agentic cluster; Bhakti yoga → Communal cluster; Jñāna yoga → Emergent cluster; Raja yoga → Structural cluster** — *"all paths lead to the same destination. Pick the one that matches your edge-weight profile."* Searles notes this is the four-strategy model *"articulated in Indian philosophy three thousand years before we derived it from reproductive biology,"* and offers the honest fork himself: *"Either the framework is rediscovering something real, or pattern-matching is more seductive than we think. Both options should be taken seriously."*

### Taoism — the Symbol primitive cannot encode Being
Taoism *"starts at Being and immediately confronts Language — the act of naming Being. And it stops."* The *Tao Te Ching*'s opening (*"The Tao that can be spoken is not the true Tao…"*) is read as: **the Symbol primitive (Layer 6) cannot faithfully encode the Being primitive (Layer 13).** Every representation introduces Noise; *"the map is not the territory."* Crucially this turns reflexive: *"The framework itself — including the 200 primitives, including this analysis — is a symbol system trying to encode something that precedes and exceeds symbols."*

*Wu wei* (non-doing) is read not as passivity but as *"action without the overhead of symbolic processing"* — in edge-weight terms, *"strengthening the edge from Being directly to Act, bypassing the intermediate nodes of Symbol, Model, Plan, Intent."* The source treats Taoism as *"the most radical challenge to the entire [framework]"*: a symbol system being told it has *"already lost the thing by naming it… a finger pointing at the moon. Don't mistake the finger for the moon."* Searles' one-word reply: *"Fair point."* (This is the same epistemic guardrail that anchors [[the-cult-test]]: the framework as tool, not truth.)

## Where the mystics meet — a prediction, flagged as such

From the six paths, the post derives a **prediction**: *"if all religious traditions start from the same root and trace different paths, then practitioners who follow any path far enough should converge."* The paths diverge in the doctrinal/legal/social/ethical middle layers (Layers 2–10) but should converge at the extremes — at the root (Being) and at the deepest experiential encounter with it. The mapping offered: Sufism (Islam), Kabbalah (Judaism), Christian mysticism (Meister Eckhart, John of the Cross, Teresa of Ávila), Zen (Buddhism), Advaita Vedanta (Hinduism) — all read as describing *"an encounter with something beyond the node, beyond the edges, beyond the graph itself."* The framework's gloss: *"the root node is the root node regardless of which path you took to get there. Being is Being. The strange loop loops regardless of where you enter it."*

A corollary the post draws: mystics are *"often persecuted by their own institutions"* (Sufis, Kabbalists, Eckhart) because the institution *"lives in the middle layers"* while the mystic *"returns to the root and reports that the middle layers are not ultimate."*

> ⚠ **Fail-legible — the mystic-convergence claim is asserted, not demonstrated.** The source states the framework "makes" this prediction and that it "appears to be borne out," but offers illustration, not evidence — and explicitly concedes that *"mystical experience is [not] identical across traditions — the vocabulary, the context, the interpretation all differ."* The underlying thesis (a perennial common core to mysticism) is **contested in religious studies**, and the post does not engage that scholarship. Carried here because the source carries it; labelled a prediction, not a finding.

## Theodicy — the same lens, six different operations

A companion section ("Theodicy in Primitive Terms") runs the problem of evil through the same six traditions, each rendered as a different operation on the primitives:

- **Christianity:** Harm is the price of **Agency** (Layer 1, Choice) — *"a graph without Harm would be a graph without Choice."*
- **Islam:** Governance operates at graph scale; the node lacks the instrumentation to evaluate graph-level design — *"the Book of Job argument… you don't have the InstrumentationSpec to run CoverageCheck on the architect's decisions."*
- **Buddhism:** *"There is no architect. The question is malformed."* Harm exists because Attachment generates the illusion of a self that can be harmed; dissolve the self and the problem disappears.
- **Judaism:** The covenant promises *"Presence through Harm,"* not absence of it — a claim about the **Attunement** primitive (the Bond is maintained *through* suffering).
- **Hinduism:** The architect *experiences* harm *as* others, because architect and nodes are *"the same Being viewed at different scales"* — Harm is Being experiencing its own Finitude.

The source's conclusion is not a resolution but a tension claim: *"Harm and Moral Status are in permanent, irreducible tension"* — an instance of [[the-permanent-tensions]], not a theodicy solved. (Note: the Theodicy section omits a distinct Taoism entry; only five traditions are given an evil-answer here, against six in the paths. Stated, not silently smoothed.)

## What the source is actually doing (and what it admits)

This article sits inside [[the-cult-test]] — a self-administered check on whether the framework has become a totalising explanation. That context is the point: the religion mapping is the most explanatory-looking, and therefore the most suspect, application in the arc. Three admissions from the source corpus are load-bearing and must travel with the mapping:

- **Self-administered, scope-anxious.** In the later external-audit post ("The Audit", 2026-03-01), the author records that **Claude "was uncomfortable with the scope of the religion mapping… Mapping six world religions through a framework developed over one weekend felt presumptuous. That discomfort stayed in the post as part of the cult test."** ([Searles-Audit])
- **Flagged by an outside critic as the canonical over-reach case.** The same external analysis singles out the expanding domain — *"Post 1: Failure tracing… Post 9: World religions. The framework expands to explain increasingly broad domains. This is either evidence of fundamental insight or evidence of a framework abstract enough to project onto anything."* ([Searles-Audit]) The religion mapping is named as Exhibit A for the "projectable onto anything" worry.
- **The framework's own guardrails apply.** Religion is read against a framework that explicitly **cannot derive Being** (one of [[three-irreducibles|the three irreducibles]]), and whose author insists *"the 200 primitives are fingers pointing at reality. They are not reality."* The Taoism reading above is the framework conceding this from inside the mapping.

> ⚠ **Fail-legible — do not promote this to a theory of religion.** Every claim here is one source's worked analogy, produced (the author states) over a single weekend in a self-described non-neutral state, in collaboration with an AI "trained to tell me things that feel insightful whether or not they are." The mappings are reported faithfully; their *truth* is unestablished and, per the source's own test design, deliberately left open. Thin where thin: there is exactly **one** source for the substance — the Searles "Cult Test" post — corroborated only by the same author's later posts; no independent scholarship is cited.
